Who am I?

I am JV. Who I am today is a person who is looking for purpose. I am a person who wants to make a positive difference, this is why I studied Environmental Science and that my previous role was in ecological restoration. During that time, my goal was to give back to nature and to preserve our taonga. A goal I share with my partner is buying and settling on old farm land and regrowing/revegetating back the land to restore native bush. I want to give back to the community by sharing my knowledge with ecological restoration and helping out practically.

I like helping others out, I get a sense of fulfilment when others and people I care about achieve their goals. I think I am a caring person; I’d like to think that I would always put others before myself in situations. I’m a strong believer in treating others with respect and always trying to see the good in others.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths:

  • Empathy: I like to put myself in others people’s shoes and see from their POV. I like understanding other people’s views and how they think.
  • Honesty: I hate lying, I am a bad liar. I like being honest with everyone even if it means telling them the harsh truth. There’s too much bullshit in the world and too many lies and fake things, this may be a reason for why I seek and value truth.
  • Can see the bigger picture/Deep thinker: I like thinking in a philosophical manner and always think of the bigger picture. I tend to ask the question why a lot because I want to understand things at a deeper level. Why are things the way they are.
  • Always craving knowledge: I love learning new things which is why I am also doing this course. I am a person that gets bored easily and believe that we learn new things all throughout our lifetime. I think I got taught this mindset from a young age by my grandparents.

Weaknesses:

  • Too hard on myself: Sometimes I think that I am too hard on myself. I criticize myself for not achieving or understanding things even though I know that people learn/work on different levels. I wouldn’t say I have high expectations for myself but I definitely do have expectations. I find it hard to forgive myself sometimes when I make a mistake.
  • Afraid to fail: I was always afraid to fail which stopped me from trying new things and getting better at them. I only realised in the last couple of years that failing at something is the first step to succeeding in it. This weakness stems from me being too hard on myself.
  • Perfectionist/OCD/Lack of fluidity: I am a creature of habit. I don’t have OCD but maybe just a little bit. I like things to be organized and have some sort of schedule for the day/week. I dislike having my schedule disrupted and find it hard getting back on track sometimes. I am definitely working on being more fluid. Identity questions

  • Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

    • In my previous role, I was a Team Leader for an Ecological Restoration Team. This meant hard physical work most days. I had one team member who, not only struggled with the work but also disliked it. My ethical dilemmas was “do I tell the big boss about this person’s work quality” and “do I talk to this person about their work quality”. As a team leader it is my job to talk to both the boss and the team member but it didn’t make talking to either about the problem any easier. I ended up talking to the team member first and asking about what they are feeling and their thoughts about this type of work. I told them that this sort of work is hard (both physical and mentally taxing) and said that not everyone is cut out for this job and would even enjoy it. I told them that I am here to help them out as much as I can and help them appreciate why we do this sort of work.

    A couple months have passed but the quality of work for this team member has not improved but actually dropped even further. This is when I decided to talk to the boss as it has affected the whole team. The team basically had to pick up this persons weight and work even harder. Our work relies on team work and there were tight deadlines on sites to be finished. I told the boss about how this person has been working, about how I tried my best in helping them and how they have affected the team morale. My boss was appreciated with me telling them about this person and in the end they ended up leaving on their own decision. My worry was that that team member would get mad at me for reporting to the boss on their work even though reporting back to the boss was part of my job. I truly hate conflict and would rather solve the problem right when it occurs. I tried my best to help that individual but the job was just not fit for them. I’m happy they left on their own will and avoided the situation where the boss talked to them about their performance.

  • Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

    • I am Filipino and I grew up in the Philippines. I moved to New Zealand when I was 10 years old which is great because I got to experience growing up in both places. As you know, the Philippines is a third world country with a lot of poverty. I grew up witnessing this which made me more thankful and appreciative for what I have. I think this is also why I am empathetic as I know that people come from different backgrounds and have different point of views. The Filipino culture has a strong sense of being family oriented. Children tend to stay and live at the family home in order to look after their parents when they get older and even give their parents money not in terms of paying rent or anything, but as a way of appreciation and looking after them. I definitely have a strong connection to my family but I also have adapted the Western way of living so I’d like to think I am a good mix of both things.

  • Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

    • As mentioned before, I was a team leader for an ecological restoration company. There were times where my team members had low motivation/low productivity and were resistant. It definitely takes a lot for me to bring morale and productivity up because during those times, I too can admit having low motivation.
